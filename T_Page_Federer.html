Stefan Zweig

Stefan Zweig was a renowned Austrian writer who vividly depicted Vienna's cultural and intellectual life in the early 20th century. His deep insights into the human psyche are evident in works such as "Beware of Pity" and "The World of Yesterday."

Arthur Schnitzler

Arthur Schnitzler, a contemporary of Freud, explored the complex inner lives of Viennese society with his narrative style. His novella "Dream Story" was set in Vienna and explores themes of love, fidelity, and the unconscious.

Ödön von Horvarth

Ödön von Horváth (1901–1938) stands out as one of the most insightful playwrights and novelists of the 20th century, whose works critically reflect the socio-political turmoil of his time. Born in Fiume (now Rijeka, Croatia), Horváth spent a significant part of his life and career in Vienna, a city that profoundly influenced his literary output.
